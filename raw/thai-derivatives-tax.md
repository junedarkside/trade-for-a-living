# Thai Derivatives Tax — Research Notes

Thai tax on derivatives is governed by **Section 40(5)** of the Thai
Revenue Code (capital gains on sale of shares, bonds, debentures, bills
of exchange, **derivatives**). Treatment differs by investor type and
underlying.

> **Caveat**: tax rules change via Royal Decree / cabinet decisions.
> Confirm current treatment with a Thai tax advisor before acting.

## Standard buckets

| Section | Covers | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| 40(2) | Employment income | n/a for trading |
| 40(4) | Professional fees / liberal professions | n/a for trading |
| **40(5)** | **Capital gains on securities + derivatives** | **Governing bin for traders** |

## Capital gains on Thai-listed securities [^1]

| Investor type | Capital gains |
|--------------|---------------|
| Thai individual (resident) | **Tax-exempt** |
| Thai juristic | No WHT; corporate income tax applies at entity rate |
| Foreign individual (non-business in Thailand) | **Tax-exempt** |
| Foreign juristic (non-business) | **15% WHT** |

> Direct quote (SET tax page): *"Thai/Foreign investors doing business in
> Thailand: Individual: Tax exempt. Juristic: No withholding tax;
> corporate income tax applies per law... Foreign investors not doing
> business in Thailand: Individual: Tax-exempt. Juristic: 15%
> withholding tax."* [^1]

## Derivatives-specific treatment

- TFEX-traded derivatives (S50 futures/options, SSF, USD/THB, gold,
  sector) governed by **Section 40(5)**.
- **Thai resident individuals**: TFEX profits **assessable income** under
  Section 40(5); the securities exemption does NOT extend to derivatives.
  Include in annual PND 90 / 91 filing.
- **Thai juristic**: corporate income tax applies (no WHT at source).
- **Foreign non-resident juristic**: **15% WHT** on gains.

## NVDR dividend WHT [^2]

| Holder | Dividend WHT |
|--------|--------------|
| NVDR holder (Thai or foreign, individual or juristic) | **10% WHT** |
| Ordinary share — Thai individual | 0% (BOI exemption for certain cases) / 10% standard |
| Ordinary share — foreign juristic (DTA country) | 5% inter-corporate or treaty rate |

> *"NVDR holders do not have voting rights, which affects their tax
> treatment under Thai law."* — loses BOI exemption eligibility. [^2]

## Stamp duty

- **Shares/debentures**: THB 1 per THB 1,000 on certificate transfers.
  **Exempt for listed securities where TSD is registrar** (SET scripless
  shares carry no stamp duty). [^1]
- **TFEX derivatives**: historically **stamp duty exempt via Royal Decree**
  (Section 99 of the Revenue Code). Treat as effectively zero; verify
  against current Royal Decree (renewed periodically).

## VAT

**7% VAT** on brokerage service fees and commissions. [^1]

## DTA relief

Thailand has DTAs with 57 countries. Capital-gains exemption under treaty
applies for:

- 14 countries **unconditionally** (Germany, Singapore, France, Denmark, …)
- 16 countries **with conditions**
- **US, UK, Japan NOT exempt** (in 27-country non-exempt list). [^1]

## Tax reporting

- **WHT on Thai-resident individual derivatives gains**: included in
  annual PND 90 / 91 (since resident individuals are not exempt on
  derivatives).
- **WHT at source on foreign-investor securities gains**: broker/TCH
  handles remittance; recipient can credit WHT against home-country
  liability (if DTA) or file for refund (Section 50(2)(e)).

## Practical trader implications

| Cadence | Action |
|---------|--------|
| **Daily** | TFEX trading P&L does **not** auto-clear tax — keep your own trade log |
| **Monthly** | Brokers issue WHT certificates for any tax withheld; reconcile against broker statement |
| **Annual** | Thai-resident individuals holding derivatives **must** include realised gains in PND 90 / 91. DTA-eligible foreigners filing refund should engage Thai tax advisor by Q1 of following year |
