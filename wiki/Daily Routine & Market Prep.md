# Daily Routine & Market Prep

A consistent daily routine reduces decision fatigue, prevents impulsive
trades, and keeps you aligned with your edge.

## Pre-Market Checklist (T-12h to T-1h before open)

| Time | Check |
|------|-------|
| **T-12h** | US overnight: S&P futures, Nasdaq, yields, DXY, oil, gold, BTC |
| **T-8h** | Asia overnight: Nikkei, Hang Seng, China, USD/JPY |
| **T-4h** | SET news: corporate actions, dividends, broker reports |
| **T-2h** | Economic calendar: BOT, Fed speakers, US data releases today |
| **T-1h** | Open-position health check; stale GTC orders audit |

## SET-Specific Timing

| Window | ICT | Quality |
|--------|-----|---------|
| Pre-open auction | 09:30–10:00 | Limited liquidity |
| Open auction | 10:00 | Volatile open |

For TFEX: day session 09:45–16:55 (S50, sector), night session
18:50–03:00 (USD/THB, gold). See [[TFEX Market Structure]].

## Pre-Open Prep (10 min)

1. **Levels**: identify support / resistance / OI walls (see [[Options Chain]]).
2. **2 scenarios max**: bullish / bearish. Don't chase 5 outcomes.
3. **Sized risk**: position size pre-computed for each scenario (see [[Position Sizing Frameworks]]).
4. **Watchlist**: 3–5 instruments. Not 30.
5. **Alerts**: pre-set price alerts on key levels.

## Open Execution Rules

- **No first 15 min** except for gap-fill setups (often profitable to fade the open).
- **Defined triggers**: "enter if SET50 breaks 25,600 with volume."
- **No chasing**: if you miss the entry, wait for pullback or skip.
- **Limit orders > market orders** (see [[Order Types]]).

## Mid-Day Review (12:30 or 14:00)

| Check | Action |
|-------|--------|
| **MTM vs plan** | Is P&L tracking expected? |
| **Position health** | Any strikes breached? Greeks blown up? |
| **Hedge check** | Delta / gamma / vega within band? |
| **Discipline** | Have you broken any rule today? |
| **News check** | Any macro events while you were trading? |

## Close Routine (16:30+)

1. **Stops updated** — if not already automated, set for overnight.
2. **Journal entry** — every trade today, screenshot attached (see [[Trade Journaling]]).
3. **Watchlist updated** — levels / OI for tomorrow.
4. **Emotional rating** — 1–5. **If you ended the day frustrated /
   revenge-trading / chasing, that affects tomorrow's mindset.**

## Weekend Review (Saturday, 2–3h)

| Activity | Time |
|----------|------|
| Equity curve review | 30 min |
| Strategy-level P&L attribution | 60 min (see [[P&L Attribution]]) |
| Regime check: trending / range / volatile? | 15 min |
| Rule retire / promote / revise | 30 min |
| Reading + journaling | 30 min |

## Mark Douglas's Five Fundamental Truths

From *Trading in the Zone* (1990). The mental model underlying all trading:

1. **Anything can happen.** No trade outcome is "impossible."
2. **You don't need to know what's going to happen next to make money.**
   You need an edge + risk management.
3. **There is a random distribution between wins and losses for any
   given edge.** Losses are normal — not a sign of failure.
4. **The market is reflexive.** It reflects the consensus of all
   participants at any moment.
5. **Every moment is unique.** Today's tape is not yesterday's.

> **Synthesis:** *"Risk comes from not knowing what you're doing."*
> Define your edge; risk what you can survive; let the distribution work.
